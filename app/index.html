7 Mistakes That Cost Roulette Players Money

Of all the casino games in existence, roulette is among the easiest and most entertaining. The game appeals to inexperienced gamblers mainly due to its simplicity.

However, even the most experienced of gamblers find certain aspects of the game enjoyable. All in all, roulette is one of the most popular games at the casino.

While roulette’s odds are among the better games in any casino, it’s still possible to lose. Additionally, just because the game is known to be easy doesn’t mean gamblers aren’t prone to mistakes.

If roulette piques your interest, here are some of the most common mistakes that cost roulette players money.

1 ‒ How Do You Play Roulette?

Before we begin, it’s essential to make sure you know the basics of roulette. When you approach a roulette table, you’ll be greeted by a large wheel with slots numbered 0 through 36. Some versions of roulette feature both a 0 and a 00, for a total of 37 or 38 slots. Additionally, each number alternates between red and black.

The dealer will spin the wheel in one direction and slide a small white ball in the other. Wherever the ball lands on the wheel is the winning number.

Gamblers put their money on the table and receive chips from the dealer. To bet, players take these chips and place them around the table to designate their wager. Gamblers can either bet on a certain number, the number’s color, and whether the number is even or odd.

Bets on solo numbers payout 35 to 1 while bets on color and even vs. odd pay out 1 to 1. As I said, the game is very straightforward and can be learned in a matter of minutes. If you are at the casino and still feel uncomfortable, watch a few spins, and you’ll get a better feel for the flow of the game.

2 ‒Thinking You’re Due for a Win

Seeing as the results of roulette are entirely random, it stands to reason that gamblers should avoid predicting future outcomes.

While you’d be correct in your assumption, it certainly doesn’t stop them from trying. Every time I visit casinos, I make a point to swing by the roulette table. When I was a younger gambler, roulette was my favorite game and I still enjoy playing from time to time.

At the roulette tables, I typically here the same types of illogical statements:
  • “I haven’t won in five spins: I’m due for a win.”
  • “The ball hasn’t landed on red for a while so I’m going to bet red.”
  • “The last five spins have all been even, it has to hit odd at some point.”
  • “Man, I haven’t seen a zero in so long, I bet that will hit in the next few spins.”
